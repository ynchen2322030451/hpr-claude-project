# Phase 1.7 — Synthetic Observations Closed-Loop Statement

## Context

The posterior calibration benchmark uses "observations" that are NOT real experimental measurements. They are generated by:
1. Selecting 18 test-split samples (known true parameters)
2. Running the true parameters through the HF coupled OpenMC-FEniCS solver to obtain outputs
3. Adding 2% multiplicative Gaussian noise to simulate measurement uncertainty

This is a **closed-loop synthetic validation**: the same simulation model that generated the training data also generates the "observations." The posterior is evaluated by comparing recovered parameters against the known ground truth — a luxury unavailable with real experimental data.

This must be disclosed prominently so reviewers do not mistake the benchmark for field-data validation.

---

## EN — Methods Section: Posterior Calibration Protocol

We evaluate posterior calibration through a closed-loop synthetic benchmark. Eighteen cases are drawn from the held-out test split (6 low-stress, 6 near-threshold, 6 high-stress) with known true input parameters. Synthetic observations are generated by evaluating the coupled OpenMC-FEniCS simulation at these true parameters and adding 2% multiplicative Gaussian noise to each output quantity, simulating realistic measurement uncertainty without introducing model-form discrepancy between the data-generating process and the surrogate's training distribution.

For each case, Metropolis-Hastings MCMC (4 independent chains, 8000 iterations per chain, burn-in 2000, thinning factor 5) samples the posterior distribution over four material parameters conditional on the synthetic observations and the BNN surrogate likelihood. Convergence is assessed via the split-rank R-hat diagnostic (max R-hat = 1.010 across all cases) and effective sample size (ESS > 350 per parameter).

This closed-loop design enables unambiguous evaluation of the surrogate-based inference pipeline — posterior coverage, bias, and contraction can be assessed against known ground truth. However, it does not test robustness to model-form error (the discrepancy between the simulation and physical reality), which remains an important direction for future validation with experimental data.

## EN — Limitations Item

The posterior calibration benchmark is a closed-loop synthetic validation: observations are generated by the same simulation model used to train the surrogate, with added noise but no model-form discrepancy. Consequently, the reported coverage (0.861) and contraction ratios reflect the best-case scenario in which the forward model is perfectly specified. Real experimental validation would introduce model-form error between simulation and measurement, potentially degrading posterior accuracy. Extending this framework to experimental observations from prototypic heat pipe reactor experiments is a necessary next step.

## CN — Methods Section: Posterior Calibration Protocol

我们通过闭环合成基准（closed-loop synthetic benchmark）评估后验标定性能。从留出测试集中抽取18个案例（低应力6个、近阈值6个、高应力6个），其真实输入参数已知。合成观测值通过在真实参数处运行耦合OpenMC-FEniCS仿真并对每个输出量添加2%乘性高斯噪声生成，模拟现实测量不确定性，但不引入数据生成过程与代理模型训练分布之间的模型形式偏差（model-form discrepancy）。

对每个案例，采用Metropolis-Hastings MCMC（4条独立链，每链8000次迭代，预烧期2000，稀疏因子5）在合成观测和BNN代理似然条件下对四个材料参数的后验分布进行采样。收敛性通过split-rank R-hat诊断（所有案例最大R-hat = 1.010）和有效样本量（ESS > 350每参数）评估。

这一闭环设计使得代理推断流程的评估清晰无歧义——后验覆盖率、偏差和收缩比均可对照已知真值进行评价。然而，该设计未测试对模型形式误差（仿真与物理现实之间的偏差）的鲁棒性，这仍是未来利用实验数据进行验证的重要方向。

## CN — Limitations Item

后验标定基准属于闭环合成验证：观测值由训练代理模型所用的同一仿真模型生成，仅添加噪声而无模型形式偏差。因此，所报告的覆盖率（0.861）和收缩比反映了前向模型完全正确指定时的最佳情形。真实实验验证将引入仿真与测量之间的模型形式误差，可能降低后验精度。将本框架扩展至原型热管反应堆的实验观测是必要的下一步工作。
