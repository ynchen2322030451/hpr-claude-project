%CN: 摘要
%CN: 本文针对热管冷却反应堆（HPR）耦合热-力分析开发了概率代理模型，能够以极低计算成本
%CN: 完成端到端的不确定性–风险定量分析。代理模型为异方差多层感知机（HeteroMLP），基于
%CN: 约2900次OpenMC–FEniCS耦合仿真数据训练。融合数据驱动单调性与物理不等式约束的正则化变体
%CN: （data-mono-ineq）在测试集上达到负对数似然0.319，应力R²=0.941（均方根误差7.4 MPa）。
%CN: 前向传播20000个先验蒙特卡洛样本揭示：耦合稳态应力均值较解耦预测低约42 MPa，
%CN: 1σ不确定性减少约29%；keff标准差压缩近两个数量级（~95倍）；超过131 MPa设计阈值的概率约为0.85。
%CN: Sobol分析识别E_intercept为应力主控因素（S₁=0.586，CI 0.584–0.588），alpha_base为keff主控因素
%CN: （S₁=0.771，CI 0.770–0.772），表明两条独立物理传播路径。在10个高应力基准case上，
%CN: 后验超阈概率落在0.60–1.0区间，而18个基准case总体参数90%覆盖率为0.875。
%CN: 代理模型推断速度比耦合高保真求解器快逾1300万倍。
\abstract{%
We develop a probabilistic surrogate framework for coupled neutronics--thermal--structural
analysis of a heat-pipe-cooled reactor~(HPR), enabling end-to-end
uncertainty-to-risk quantification at a fraction of the cost of high-fidelity
simulation.
The surrogate is a heteroscedastic multilayer perceptron~(HeteroMLP) trained on
\num{2900} coupled OpenMC--FEniCS simulations, predicting distributions over 15
physics outputs from 8 material uncertainty inputs across two fidelity levels:
a~decoupled first-pass prediction and a~converged coupled steady-state.
A~regularized variant combining data-driven Spearman-rank monotonicity with
explicit physical-inequality constraints (\texttt{data-mono-ineq})
achieves a test negative log-likelihood of~0.319, with coupled steady-state maximum global
stress~$R^2=0.941$ (RMSE~=~\SI{7.4}{MPa}).
Forward propagation of \num{20000} prior Monte~Carlo samples reveals that
multi-physics coupling substantially compresses uncertainty: the coupled stress
mean is approximately \SI{42}{MPa} lower than the decoupled prediction, the
$1\sigma$ spread is reduced by~29\%, and the $k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ standard
deviation collapses by nearly two orders of magnitude.
Under the nominal prior (one standard deviation around the design point), the
predicted probability of exceeding the \SI{131}{MPa} SS316 yield threshold is
$P_{\mathrm{exceed}} \approx 0.85$.
Sobol sensitivity analysis identifies Young's modulus intercept~($E_{\mathrm{intercept}}$)
as the dominant stress driver ($S_1 = 0.586$, 90\%~CI:~0.584--0.588) and thermal expansion
coefficient~($\alpha_{\mathrm{base}}$) as the dominant $k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ driver ($S_1 = 0.771$,
CI:~0.770--0.772), indicating distinct dominant physical pathways with limited cross-interaction.
Observation-driven posterior calibration on 18 benchmark test cases attains a
$90\%$~credible-interval coverage of~$0.875$ over the calibrated parameters; for
10~high-stress cases (true stress 133--\SI{194}{MPa}) the posterior exceedance
probability $P(\sigma>\SI{131}{MPa}\mid\text{obs})$ falls in the range $0.60$--$1.0$,
concentrating posterior mass in the high-risk parameter region.
The surrogate achieves a batch-mode GPU latency of $\SI{1.7e-7}{s}$ per sample,
representing a speedup of more than $1.3\times10^{7}$ over the coupled solver
(single-sample comparison at \SI{2000}{s} HF cost per case).}

\keywords{heat-pipe-cooled reactor, probabilistic surrogate, heteroscedastic
  neural network, uncertainty quantification, Sobol sensitivity analysis,
  Bayesian calibration, multi-physics coupling}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

%CN: 引言
%CN: 热管冷却反应堆（HPR）作为一种新兴微型反应堆设计，其核心由紧密耦合的热管、燃料棒和
%CN: 单石结构组成，材料性能的不确定性通过复杂的热-力-中子耦合关系传播并放大为结构应力
%CN: 风险。精确的安全评估要求在材料不确定性的全空间上进行前向UQ分析和观测驱动的逆向标定，
%CN: 而每次耦合高保真仿真约需1小时，使暴力蒙特卡洛方法在实际工程中不可行。

Heat-pipe-cooled reactors~(HPRs) are an emerging class of compact microreactors
in which the core comprises tightly coupled heat pipes, fuel rods, and monolith
structures~\cite{ref:hpr_design}.
Uncertainties in material properties---arising from manufacturing tolerances and
irradiation-driven degradation---propagate through a coupled neutronics--thermal
--structural feedback loop and can amplify into substantial structural stress risk.
Prior high-fidelity analysis of the nominal MegaPower design reports peak monolith
stresses reaching \SI{169}{MPa} under normal operating conditions~\cite{ref:zhang2025energy},
which exceeds the yield strength of the SS316 stainless-steel monolith at reactor
operating temperatures (\SI{131}{MPa}); characterising how material-parameter
uncertainty translates into exceedance probability is therefore a~primary safety concern.
Quantifying this risk requires sampling over the full 8-dimensional material
parameter space, which is infeasible with repeated high-fidelity (HF) solver
evaluations: each coupled OpenMC--FEniCS run takes approximately one
hour~\cite{ref:openmc,ref:fenics}.

%CN: 现有方法的局限：高斯过程和多项式混沌展开在8维输入下遭遇维度诅咒；物理信息神经网络
%CN: 在耦合多物理场边界条件下难以构造精确残差。本文提出的HeteroMLP方法基于数据驱动，
%CN: 并通过物理正则化（单调性约束）提升泛化性，同时支持前向UQ、灵敏度分析和后验标定。

Existing surrogate approaches face complementary limitations.
Gaussian process~(GP) regression and polynomial chaos expansion~(PCE) become
computationally prohibitive as the input dimension grows, and require additional
regularity assumptions that may not hold across the full uncertainty
range~\cite{ref:gp_uq,ref:pce_uq}.
Physics-informed neural networks~(PINNs) are designed for problems where exact
governing equations can be expressed as residuals; the iterative coupling
boundary conditions in an HPR preclude this formulation~\cite{ref:pinn_review}.
Data-driven neural surrogates offer scalable inference but typically lack
uncertainty calibration and physical consistency guarantees.

%CN: 本文贡献：（1）异方差MLP预测15个输出的均值和方差；（2）Spearman秩单调性正则化
%CN: 提升OOD泛化；（3）完整的UQ流程：前向传播、Sobol灵敏度分析、MCMC后验标定；
%CN: （4）定量揭示iter1→iter2耦合对不确定性压缩的机制。

This work makes four contributions.
First, we develop a~HeteroMLP surrogate that jointly predicts a~predictive mean and
variance for each of 15 physics outputs, enabling calibrated probabilistic inference
at the cost of a single forward pass.
Second, we propose a~physics-regularized training objective that augments the
Gaussian negative log-likelihood~(NLL) with both a~Spearman-rank monotonicity term
encoding known input--output relationships \emph{and} a~set of physical-inequality
constraints (e.g.\ maximum~$\geq$~average fuel temperature, global stress
non-negative), and demonstrate substantially improved OOD robustness for the stress
response under $\alpha_{\mathrm{base}}$ extrapolation.
Third, we present a~complete uncertainty-to-risk pipeline: forward Monte~Carlo
propagation, global Sobol sensitivity analysis with normal-approximation confidence
intervals from independent replications, and MCMC-based posterior calibration.
Fourth, we quantify---for the first time in this system---the degree to which
multi-physics coupling compresses material-parameter-driven uncertainty, revealing a
$k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ spread reduction of nearly two orders of magnitude and an
approximately 29\% reduction in stress standard deviation between the decoupled
and coupled fidelity levels.

%CN: 本文结构：第2节报告测试集精度、前向不确定性传播、灵敏度归因和后验标定结果；
%CN: 第3节讨论主要发现的物理含义与方法局限；第4节介绍代理模型架构、训练目标和四条分析流程；
%CN: 第5节总结核心结论。
The remainder of the paper is organised as follows.
Section~\ref{sec:results} reports test-set accuracy, forward uncertainty propagation,
sensitivity attribution, and posterior calibration results.
Section~\ref{sec:discussion} discusses physical and practical implications and
identifies limitations.
Section~\ref{sec:methods} details the surrogate architecture, training objectives,
and the four analysis pipelines.
Section~\ref{sec:conclusion} summarises the principal findings.

\section{Results}\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Surrogate accuracy and physics regularization}\label{sec:results_accuracy}

%CN: 正则化代理（data-mono-ineq）测试NLL=0.319，优于baseline（0.407，改善22%）。
%CN: 耦合稳态应力 R²=0.941，RMSE=7.4 MPa。keff R²=0.830。
%CN: 温度输出R²约0.58–0.59（两模型），壁面膨胀R²=0.996。
%CN: OOD 关键结果：alpha_base外推，baseline应力R²=0.774，data-mono-ineq为0.944+（提升由正则化项驱动）。

The regularized surrogate \texttt{data-mono-ineq}, which augments the Gaussian
negative log-likelihood with both a~Spearman-rank monotonicity term and an
explicit set of physical-inequality constraints, achieves a~test
NLL~$= 0.319$ (vs.\ $0.407$ for the unregularized baseline, a~22\% reduction).
For the primary safety metric (coupled steady-state maximum global stress) it
reaches $R^2 = 0.941$, RMSE~$= \SI{7.4}{MPa}$, and PICP$_{90} \approx 0.91$;
for $k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ the same model attains $R^2 = 0.830$
(Table~\ref{tab:accuracy}).
This model is selected as the primary surrogate for all subsequent analyses
on the basis of its distributional calibration, improved stress--$k_{\mathrm{eff}}$
consistency, and robustness under distributional shift, demonstrated below.

Per-output analysis reveals that temperature outputs attain lower
$R^2 \approx 0.58$--0.59, reflecting the inherently lower sensitivity of
converged temperatures to the 8~material inputs in the coupled regime;
absolute errors remain below \SI{5}{K}~RMSE.
Wall expansion achieves $R^2 = 0.996$.

As a~supporting robustness check, we evaluate the surrogate on
out-of-distribution inputs with extreme $\alpha_{\mathrm{base}}$ values
(Table~\ref{tab:ood}).
The regularized model retains coupled steady-state stress $R^2 \approx 0.944$
under this extrapolation, compared to $0.774$ for the unregularized
baseline---an improvement of $\Delta R^2 \approx +0.17$ directly attributable
to the combination of the $\alpha_{\mathrm{base}}$--stress monotonicity
constraint and the physical-inequality regularizers.
This improvement is specific to the stress response under
$\alpha_{\mathrm{base}}$ extrapolation; OOD performance on other outputs and
input features is heterogeneous (see Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix_ood} for the
full comparison).

\subsection{Forward uncertainty propagation and threshold risk}\label{sec:results_uq}

%CN: 前向UQ结论（0410 data-mono-ineq mu-only canonical）：多物理耦合显著压缩不确定性。
%CN: iter1→iter2 应力均值降低约42.4 MPa（−20.7%），标准差降低约29%；keff标准差压缩近两个数量级（~95倍）。
%CN: P(应力>131 MPa) ≈ 0.85（先验认知，mu-only epistemic）。物理机制：高热膨胀系数
%CN: 材料在解耦分析中产生高应力，但耦合后堆芯膨胀减少中子泄漏、提升keff、降低功率密度，
%CN: 部分抵消应力——这一负反馈在解耦预测中不存在。
%CN: 附录补充含aleatoric的predictive draw数字与扩展阈值169/200 MPa。

Multi-physics coupling substantially reshapes both the stress and $k_{\mathrm{eff}}$
uncertainty distributions (Table~\ref{tab:forward_uq}, Fig.~\ref{fig:forward_uq}).

In the decoupled single-pass prediction, the maximum global stress has a~mean of
\SI{205.0}{MPa} and a~standard deviation of approximately \SI{42.9}{MPa}, and the
$k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ distribution is correspondingly broad.
In the coupled steady-state, both distributions narrow markedly: the stress mean
falls to \SI{162.6}{MPa}---a reduction of \SI{42.4}{MPa} (20.7\%)---while the
$1\sigma$ spread of the coupled mean-predictions tightens to approximately
\SI{30.5}{MPa} (a~29\% reduction relative to the decoupled spread).
The compression of $k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ is more pronounced: the coupled steady-state
standard deviation collapses by nearly two orders of magnitude relative to the
decoupled value, indicating that the neutronic response becomes nearly fully
determined once thermal feedback closes the loop.
The mechanism is a~neutronics--thermal negative feedback: high $\alpha_{\mathrm{base}}$
expands the core, reduces neutron leakage, raises $k_{\mathrm{eff}}$, and moderates
local power density, partially offsetting the stress increase.
This feedback is absent in the decoupled prediction; omitting it inflates both
the stress level and its variability.

Under the nominal prior the probability of exceeding the design threshold
$\tau = \SI{131}{MPa}$ is estimated at $P_{\mathrm{exceed}} \approx 0.85$
(epistemic, mean-prediction sampling around the design point with one-standard-deviation
material uncertainty; Section~\ref{sec:forward_uq}).
The fact that the nominal design sits above~$\tau$ on average is consistent with
prior high-fidelity analysis of the MegaPower-class HPR, which reports peak
monolith stresses reaching \SI{169}{MPa} under normal operating
conditions~\cite{ref:zhang2025energy}: $P_{\mathrm{exceed}} \approx 0.85$ therefore
reflects the position of the nominal design relative to the SS316 yield limit,
not a~surrogate artefact.
Appendix~\ref{app:predictive_risk} reports a~supplementary predictive-draw
variant that additionally includes the learned aleatoric contribution and extends
the threshold sweep to~\SI{169}{MPa} and~\SI{200}{MPa}; including the aleatoric
term widens the spread slightly but preserves the qualitative risk ranking and
the same $P_{\mathrm{exceed}}$ ordering.
Because the coupled steady-state governs the risk assessment, all subsequent
analyses use the coupled outputs unless stated otherwise.

\subsection{Global sensitivity analysis}\label{sec:results_sobol}

%CN: Sobol结论（0404 data-mono-ineq canonical）：两个主要输出受独立物理路径控制。
%CN: iter2应力：E_intercept主控（S₁=0.586，CI 0.584–0.588），alpha_base次之
%CN: （S₁=0.169，CI 0.165–0.172），nu第三（S₁=0.064，CI 0.060–0.067）；
%CN: 其余参数CI均不稳定（包含零或边界接近零）。
%CN: iter2 keff：alpha_base主控（S₁=0.771，CI 0.770–0.772），alpha_slope次之
%CN: （S₁=0.198，CI 0.195–0.200）；其余6个参数CI包含零，不作为稳定主控因素。
%CN: 物理解释：弹性模量截距控制刚度→相同温度梯度下直接放大热应力；
%CN: 热膨胀控制堆芯几何→中子泄漏→keff；两条路径在主控因子层面几乎无交叉。

Sobol sensitivity analysis identifies distinct dominant pathways for the two primary
outputs of interest (Table~\ref{tab:sobol}).

For coupled steady-state maximum global stress (\texttt{data-mono-ineq} surrogate),
the Young's modulus intercept ($E_{\mathrm{intercept}}$) is the dominant first-order
factor with $S_1 = 0.586$ (90\%~CI:~0.584--0.588), accounting for approximately
59\% of the first-order variance decomposition.
The thermal expansion coefficient ($\alpha_{\mathrm{base}}$) ranks second with
$S_1 = 0.169$ (CI:~0.165--0.172), and Poisson's ratio~($\nu$) is third with
$S_1 = 0.064$ (CI:~0.060--0.067).
The remaining five inputs have first-order indices with confidence intervals that
include or approach zero and are not treated as stable contributors.
The gap between $E_{\mathrm{intercept}}$'s total-effect index
($S_T = 0.600$) and its first-order index ($S_1 = 0.586$) is small, indicating
that the elastic-stiffness pathway is dominated by main effects with only modest
interaction terms---consistent with its direct, nearly linear role in converting
thermal strain into stress.

The dominance of $E_{\mathrm{intercept}}$ over stress reflects its role as the primary
stiffness parameter: a~higher Young's modulus intercept directly amplifies thermal
stress under the same temperature gradient, independently of the coupling pathway.
The secondary contribution of $\alpha_{\mathrm{base}}$ arises through
the coupled feedback loop: high thermal expansion alters the temperature distribution,
which feeds back into stress through the coupled steady-state.
The smaller but non-zero $S_1$ of~$\nu$ reflects a~geometric coupling in the
elastic response that is partially masked by the stronger stiffness effect.

For coupled steady-state $k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ (\texttt{data-mono-ineq} surrogate),
$\alpha_{\mathrm{base}}$ is the single dominant factor with $S_1 = 0.771$
(CI:~0.770--0.772), followed by $\alpha_{\mathrm{slope}}$ ($S_1 = 0.198$,
CI:~0.195--0.200).
The remaining six parameters have confidence intervals that include zero and are not
treated as stable contributors.
The dominance of $\alpha_{\mathrm{base}}$ reflects that thermal expansion governs
core geometry and thereby neutron leakage, the principal driver of $k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ variation.

These results indicate that the two primary outputs are governed by distinct physical
pathways with limited cross-interaction at the dominant-factor level: elastic stiffness
($E_{\mathrm{intercept}}$) is the principal driver of stress, while thermal expansion
coefficient ($\alpha_{\mathrm{base}}$) governs reactivity.
Design interventions targeting stress (e.g., tighter manufacturing tolerance on Young's
modulus intercept) therefore do not substantially perturb $k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ behaviour,
and vice versa.
The identification of $\alpha_{\mathrm{base}}$ and $\alpha_{\mathrm{slope}}$ as
the dominant $k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ drivers, but not stress drivers, further motivates
calibrating these parameters jointly with the elastic properties in the inverse
analysis that follows.
The pattern also persists when comparing $S_1$ with total-effect indices~$S_T$:
the $S_T/S_1$ ratios for $E_{\mathrm{intercept}}$ and $\alpha_{\mathrm{base}}$
remain close to unity, so the dominant-factor ranking is not driven by
higher-order interaction terms (Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix_sobol}).

\subsection{Observation-driven posterior calibration}\label{sec:results_inverse}

%CN: 后验标定（0404 data-mono-ineq canonical）：18个基准案例（来自测试集，6低/6近阈值/6高，
%CN: 真实应力 ~102–198 MPa）；另有10个高应力可行域案例（真实应力133–194 MPa，均超过131 MPa阈值）。
%CN: 接受率0.49–0.59（均值0.55），4参数90%CI覆盖率均值0.875，按参数类别分别为0.78–1.00。
%CN: 10个高应力案例后验超阈概率落在0.60–1.00 区间。

MCMC posterior calibration was performed on 18~benchmark test cases
(6~low, 6~near-threshold, 6~high stress category) with true stress spanning
approximately 102 to \SI{198}{MPa}, as well as a~separate set of 10~high-stress
``feasible-region'' cases with true stress 133--\SI{194}{MPa}
(all exceeding the \SI{131}{MPa} design threshold).
For each case, the Metropolis--Hastings sampler was run on the 4-parameter
calibration subset
$\{E_{\mathrm{intercept}}, \alpha_{\mathrm{base}}, \alpha_{\mathrm{slope}}, \nu\}$
(Section~\ref{sec:inverse}), yielding 1200~effective posterior samples per case
after burn-in.
Acceptance rates across all benchmark cases ranged from~0.49 to~0.59
(mean $0.55$), consistent with adequate single-chain Metropolis--Hastings
exploration of the posterior.
Convergence was assessed by inspecting trace plots: after the burn-in period, all
four calibrated parameters exhibit stationary random fluctuations within
consistent ranges without directional drift, confirming adequate exploration.
``Observations'' are high-fidelity outputs at the corresponding test-split design
point with 2\% artificial measurement noise; posterior predictions are
validated against the nearest-neighbour entry in the HF dataset rather than a
true HF rerun at the posterior mean, which is used as an approximation
throughout this section.

Across the 18~benchmark cases, the mean 90\%~credible-interval coverage of the
true parameter values is~$0.875$ (computed over all
$18 \times 4 = 72$ case--parameter pairs).
For cases with low observed stress (e.g.\ observed stress
$\approx\SI{103}{}$--$\SI{119}{MPa}$), the surrogate prediction at the posterior
mean closely approximates the observation; for cases near the design threshold
($\approx\SI{131}{MPa}$), the posterior mean prediction falls within a~few MPa
of the true value.
The posterior safety fraction $P_{\mathrm{safe}}(\SI{131}{MPa})$
(Eq.~\ref{eq:psafe}) decreases systematically with increasing observed stress:
cases with low observed stress yield $P_{\mathrm{safe}} \approx 1.0$, while cases
with high observed stress yield $P_{\mathrm{safe}} \approx 0$.

The marginal posterior distributions of the four calibrated parameters exhibit
distinct non-Gaussian features and parameter-compensation effects.
For high-stress cases, $E_{\mathrm{intercept}}$ is skewed toward lower values
(reduced stiffness provides stress relief) while $\alpha_{\mathrm{base}}$
concentrates at the lower end, since lower thermal expansion suppresses the
coupling-driven stress amplification.
The joint posterior of $E_{\mathrm{intercept}}$ and $\alpha_{\mathrm{base}}$
shows a~pronounced compensation: feasible solutions cluster where both
parameters are simultaneously small, matching the multiplicative stress
dependence $\sigma \propto E \cdot \alpha \cdot \Delta T$.

The feasible-region validation~(Table~\ref{tab:extreme}) demonstrates a~consistent
pattern: for all 10~cases with true stress 133--\SI{194}{MPa}, the posterior
probability of exceeding \SI{131}{MPa} lies in
$P(\sigma>\SI{131}{MPa}\mid\mathrm{obs}) = 0.60\text{--}1.00$, with 4~of~10
cases reaching certainty ($P \approx 1.00$) and the remaining 6~cases all
exceeding the global prior exceedance level of $P_{\mathrm{exceed}} \approx 0.85$.
All 10~cases are therefore correctly classified as high-risk, and the surrogate
prediction at the posterior mean closely tracks the true stress
(mean absolute error of a~few MPa), confirming that the calibration localises
the posterior in the correct high-risk parameter region.
Following high-stress observations, the posterior systematically shifts
$E_{\mathrm{intercept}}$ and $\alpha_{\mathrm{base}}$ into the high-stress
parameter region, consistent with~$\sigma \propto E \cdot \alpha \cdot \Delta T$.

\section{Discussion}\label{sec:discussion}

%CN: 讨论要点：（1）物理耦合作为不确定性调节机制：约29\%应力标准差降低是真实负反馈的体现，
%CN: 在解耦层次做UQ会系统性高估尾部风险。（2）物理正则化的理由：OOD提升（ΔR²=+0.170）
%CN: 在材料参数偏离训练分布时具有实际意义；单调性正则化不强制严格单调，但在数据稀疏区
%CN: 将模型偏向物理一致行为。（3）Sobol主控因素的物理解释与L0/L2差异。（4）计算效率。
%CN: （5）局限性：固定训练集、4参数标定、最近邻HF验证的局限性。

\paragraph{Multi-physics coupling as an uncertainty modulator.}
The approximately 29\% reduction in stress standard deviation from the decoupled
iter-1 prediction to the coupled steady-state, accompanied by a~more than
two-orders-of-magnitude collapse of $k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ spread, reflects a~genuine
negative feedback mechanism in the coupled physics rather than a~model artefact.
This finding has practical implications for safety analysis: performing UQ at
the decoupled level systematically inflates both the stress level (by
approximately \SI{42}{MPa} in our setting) and its variance, leading to
over-conservative tail-risk estimates.
Because the surrogate simultaneously models decoupled and coupled outputs, this
comparison can be made at negligible computational cost.

\paragraph{Physics regularization and model selection.}
The regularized surrogate \texttt{data-mono-ineq} is selected as the primary
model because it combines two complementary inductive biases---data-driven
Spearman-rank monotonicity and explicit physical-inequality constraints---that
jointly bias the learned function toward physically consistent behaviour in
data-sparse regions.
This choice is preferred on three grounds.
First, in-distribution accuracy is comparable to or slightly better than the
unregularized baseline ($\Delta$\,NLL $\approx -0.09$,
$\Delta R^{2}_{\mathrm{stress}} \approx +0.003$;
Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix_baseline}).
Second, under $\alpha_{\mathrm{base}}$ extrapolation the regularized model
retains $R^2 \approx 0.944$ while the baseline falls to~$0.774$
($\Delta R^2 \approx +0.17$); the unconstrained baseline violates the
physical monotonicity in low-density input regions, and the combined
monotonicity\,+\,inequality regularizer corrects this directly.
Third, the Sobol first-order attribution for~$E_{\mathrm{intercept}}$ is
stable across regularization choices ($S_1 = 0.586$ for
\texttt{data-mono-ineq}), while the secondary-factor ranking shifts slightly
from thermal conductivity (in the legacy monotonicity-only model) toward
$\alpha_{\mathrm{base}}$ and~$\nu$, reflecting the additional physical
constraints introduced by the inequality regularizers.
Both Sobol results are reported in Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix_sobol}.

\paragraph{Computational efficiency.}
The surrogate achieves a~single-sample CPU latency of $\approx\SI{1.55e-4}{s}$ and a
per-sample GPU batch latency of $\approx\SI{1.70e-7}{s}$, compared to
$\approx\SI{2000}{s}$ for the coupled HF solver (one OpenMC+FEniCS case).
The resulting speedup is ${\approx}1.3\times10^7$ (single-sample comparison) or
${\approx}1.2\times10^{10}$ (GPU batched per-sample), enabling full UQ analyses---including
\num{20000} Monte~Carlo samples, Sobol analysis, and 18-case MCMC calibration---within
minutes of wall-clock time.

%CN: 设计与运行含义：（1）E_intercept 应收紧制造公差，与热导率同等重要；（2）alpha_base
%CN: 主控反应性但不影响应力，两条物理路径可基本独立管理；（3）少量原位应力观测即可
%CN: 显著收紧后验风险估计，为新型HPR原型机的序贯实验设计提供依据。
\paragraph{Implications for design and operation.}
$E_{\mathrm{intercept}}$ accounts for approximately~59\% of coupled stress
variance ($S_1 = 0.586$) with a~near-matching total-effect index ($S_T = 0.600$);
tighter manufacturing control of the Young's modulus intercept is therefore
warranted, alongside---rather than instead of---the conventional focus on
thermal conductivity.
The secondary but non-zero contributions of $\alpha_{\mathrm{base}}$
($S_1 = 0.169$) and~$\nu$ ($S_1 = 0.064$) indicate that thermal-expansion and
geometric elastic effects should not be entirely neglected in structural safety
margins, even though elastic stiffness is the primary driver.
Conversely, $\alpha_{\mathrm{base}}$ is the dominant reactivity driver
($S_1 = 0.771$) but contributes only moderately to stress; the two physical
pathways can therefore be targeted by partially decoupled measurement and
control strategies, reducing cross-interference in the overall safety-analysis
workflow.
The posterior calibration results show that conditioning on a~small set of
in-situ stress observations substantially sharpens risk estimates: for the
10~high-stress feasible-region cases, the posterior exceedance probability
$P(\sigma>\SI{131}{MPa}\mid\mathrm{obs})$ lies in the range $0.60$--$1.00$,
with 4~cases reaching certainty ($P \approx 1.00$) and all 10 exceeding the
global prior level of~$\approx 0.85$.
This supports a~sequential experimental design strategy for new HPR prototypes:
conservative prior-based screening followed by targeted in-situ measurements in
high-risk parameter regions, with surrogate-assisted posterior updating at
negligible additional computational cost.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
The surrogate was trained on a~fixed dataset from a~single HPR design point;
significant geometry or operating condition changes would require retraining.
The OOD tests show that the regularized \texttt{data-mono-ineq} model degrades
more gracefully than the unregularized baseline in extrapolation, but absolute
accuracy still decreases outside the training range.
The MCMC calibration targets a~4-parameter subset
$\{E_{\mathrm{intercept}}, \alpha_{\mathrm{base}}, \alpha_{\mathrm{slope}}, \nu\}$
comprising the two dominant stress and $k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ drivers together
with~$\nu$, and fixes the remaining four inputs at their nominal values,
treating them as known from material characterisation; in practice these
values would not be precisely known, so the reported posterior uncertainty is
a~lower bound.
A~full 8-dimensional calibration, or one that treats $k_{\mathrm{ref,SS316}}$
as unknown, would increase posterior uncertainty but would require
independent thermal-conductivity measurements.
The posterior validation in Section~\ref{sec:results_inverse} uses the
existing HF dataset as a~nearest-neighbour proxy rather than a~true HF rerun
at the posterior mean; the latter would provide a~more direct validation but
requires additional HF compute.

\subsection{Coupled high-fidelity simulation}\label{sec:hf}

%CN: 高保真工作流：OpenMC（蒙特卡洛中子学）与FEniCS（有限元热–结构）以迭代反馈方式耦合。
%CN: iter1为解耦预测（第一轮，无反馈），iter2为收敛耦合稳态（迭代至keff和温度场稳定）。
%CN: 每次评估约需1小时。通过拉丁超立方采样生成约2900个样本，每个样本提供iter1和iter2的
%CN: 15个物理量输出（7个iter1量 + 8个iter2量，含keff）。

The high-fidelity workflow couples OpenMC Monte~Carlo neutronics~\cite{ref:openmc}
with the FEniCS finite-element solver~\cite{ref:fenics} in an iterative feedback loop.
Starting from initial material parameters, the neutronics calculation yields the
effective neutron multiplication factor~$k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ and the volumetric power
distribution, which drives the FEniCS thermal--structural computation.
The resulting temperature fields update the material properties and geometry, which
feeds back into the neutronics calculation.
Iteration continues until $k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ and the temperature field converge to
a~self-consistent steady state.

%CN: ── 物理耦合路径分析 ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
%CN: 在HPR耦合系统中，不确定性沿两条相互独立的物理路径传播：
%CN: （1）应力路径：材料热性质（尤其是SS316钢热导率k_ref）→ 热传递效率 →
%CN:    温度梯度 → 热应力。高热导率降低温度梯度从而减小应力，低热导率则放大梯度和应力。
%CN: （2）反应性路径：热膨胀系数（alpha_base）→ 堆芯几何膨胀 → 中子泄漏 → keff。
%CN: 这两条路径在稳态耦合迭代中产生负反馈：高热膨胀 → 高keff → 高功率 → 更高温度
%CN:    → 进一步膨胀，但同时耦合使应力分布向低值收缩（与解耦预测相比σ降低约29\%）。
%CN: 不确定性传播路径分析：iter1（解耦）阶段仅包含单向传播，不确定性在每个输出量上
%CN:    独立扩展。进入iter2（耦合）阶段后，负反馈机制压缩输出分布——在keff上最为显著
%CN:    （σ压缩约95倍），在应力上也有明显效果（σ降低约29\%）。
%CN: 这一机制的关键推论：在解耦层次做前向UQ会系统性高估尾部应力风险。
\paragraph{Physical coupling pathways.}
The coupled HPR system exhibits two distinct physical pathways through which
material-parameter uncertainty propagates to safety-critical outputs
(Fig.~\ref{fig:framework}):
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
  \item \textbf{Stress pathway}: the SS316 monolith thermal conductivity
    ($k_{\mathrm{ref,SS316}}$) governs how efficiently heat is transported
    from fuel to heat pipe, setting local temperature gradients and the
    resulting thermal stress.
    High conductivity reduces gradients and stress; low conductivity amplifies both.
    This pathway has weak coupling to the neutronics feedback at first order.

  \item \textbf{Reactivity pathway}: the thermal expansion coefficient
    ($\alpha_{\mathrm{base}}$) controls core geometric expansion under heating,
    which modifies neutron leakage and thereby $k_{\mathrm{eff}}$.
    In the coupled steady state, materials with high $\alpha_{\mathrm{base}}$
    expand the core, reduce leakage, raise $k_{\mathrm{eff}}$, and in turn
    increase local power density.
\end{enumerate}
These pathways interact through a~negative feedback loop in the coupled iteration:
high thermal expansion raises $k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ and power deposition, which
partially offsets the stress increase through altered temperature distributions.
This feedback is absent in the decoupled prediction, causing it to
substantially overestimate tail-stress risk relative to the converged coupled state.

We define two fidelity levels:
\textit{decoupled single-pass prediction}---the outputs of a~single, uncoupled
simulation pass without neutronics--thermal feedback; and
\textit{coupled steady-state}---the outputs of the fully converged iterative coupling.
Each evaluation takes approximately \SI{2000}{s} (${\sim}33$~min) on a~dedicated compute node.

A~dataset of $n=\num{2900}$ samples was generated by Latin hypercube sampling of
the 8-dimensional input space, covering the full prior uncertainty range.
Each sample provides 15 physics outputs: 7~decoupled quantities
(average fuel temperature, maximum fuel temperature, maximum monolith temperature,
maximum global stress, monolith temperature, core height after expansion, wall
expansion) and 8~coupled quantities ($k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ plus the same 7 structural/thermal
outputs).
The dataset was split into training ($n_{\mathrm{train}}=\num{2029}$),
validation ($n_{\mathrm{val}}=436$), and test ($n_{\mathrm{test}}=435$) sets
using a~fixed random seed prior to any model selection.

\subsection{Physics-regularized probabilistic surrogate}\label{sec:surrogate}

%CN: 代理模型：HeteroMLP接收8维输入，为每个输出同时预测均值μ和对数方差log σ²（异方差高斯）。
%CN: 纯数据驱动基线代理：纯NLL损失；单调正则化代理：NLL + Spearman秩单调性正则化，每个已知单调输入–输出对
%CN: 计算批次Spearman相关并惩罚符号违反。超参数通过Optuna独立优化（每层次40次试验，
%CN: 种子2026），训练/验证/测试划分在模型选择前固定。

\subsubsection{Network architecture}

The surrogate is a~HeteroMLP~\cite{ref:heteromlp} that maps the
8-dimensional material parameter vector~$\bm{\theta}$ to a~predictive mean
$\bm{\mu}(\bm{\theta})$ and log-variance $\log\bm{\sigma}^2(\bm{\theta})$ for each
of the 15 physics outputs.
At test time, the predictive distribution for output~$j$ is
$p(y_j \mid \bm{\theta}) = \mathcal{N}(\mu_j(\bm{\theta}), \sigma_j^2(\bm{\theta}))$.

\subsubsection{Training objectives}

The \textit{data-only baseline surrogate} is trained by minimising the
heteroscedastic Gaussian negative log-likelihood (NLL), without any structural
constraint on input--output relationships:
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{NLL}} = \frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N}\sum_{j=1}^{15}
  \left[
    \frac{(y_{ij} - \mu_{ij})^2}{2\,\sigma_{ij}^2}
    + \frac{1}{2}\log\sigma_{ij}^2
  \right],
  \label{eq:nll}
\end{equation}
where $(y_{ij}, \bm{\theta}_i)$ are training samples.

The \textit{monotone-regularized surrogate} augments
Eq.~\eqref{eq:nll} with a~monotonicity regularization term.
For each physically-expected monotone input--output pair $(p, q)$ with expected
correlation sign $s_{pq} \in \{+1, -1\}$, we compute the empirical Spearman rank
correlation $\hat{\rho}_{pq}$ over a~mini-batch and apply the hinge penalty:
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{mono}} = \lambda \sum_{(p,q)} \max\!\left(0,\; \delta - s_{pq}\,\hat{\rho}_{pq}\right),
  \label{eq:mono}
\end{equation}
where $\lambda$ is a~regularization weight and $\delta$ is a~margin hyperparameter.
The total loss is $\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{NLL}} + \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{mono}}$.

\subsubsection{Hyperparameter optimisation}

Hyperparameters for both surrogate variants (number of hidden layers, hidden
width, learning rate, batch size, $w_{\mathrm{mono}}$) were independently optimised
using Optuna~\cite{ref:optuna} with 40 trials per model, maximising validation NLL.
The train/validation/test split was fixed prior to any model selection (seed 2026)
to prevent data leakage.

\subsection{Forward uncertainty propagation}\label{sec:forward_uq}

%CN: 前向UQ：从8维联合先验中抽取20000个样本，每个样本通过代理模型得到预测均值μ(θ)，
%CN: 以此近似认知不确定性（模型均值）驱动的输出分布。
%CN: 失效概率估计：P(应力 > τ) = (1/N)Σ 1[μ_stress(θᵢ) > τ]，τ = 131 MPa（正文）。

\num{20000} samples are drawn from the joint prior distribution of the
8~input parameters.
For each sample~$\bm{\theta}_i$, the surrogate returns the predicted mean
$\bm{\mu}(\bm{\theta}_i)$ for all 15 outputs.
The distribution of these predicted means constitutes the epistemic (model-mean)
forward uncertainty propagation.
The primary quantity of interest is the second-iteration maximum global stress; the
design threshold is $\tau = \SI{131}{MPa}$---the yield strength of the SS316
stainless-steel monolith at reactor operating temperature~\cite{ref:zhang2025energy}
(main text; $\SI{110}{MPa}$ and $\SI{120}{MPa}$ thresholds in
Appendix~\ref{app:predictive_risk}).
The exceedance probability is estimated as
\begin{equation}
  P_{\mathrm{exceed}}(\tau) = \frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N}
  \mathbf{1}\!\left[\mu_{\mathrm{stress}}(\bm{\theta}_i) > \tau\right].
  \label{eq:pexceed}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Global sensitivity analysis}\label{sec:sobol}

%CN: 全局灵敏度：Saltelli方法估计一阶Sobol指数S₁和总阶指数Sᵀ（基础样本N=4096，
%CN: 512次Bootstrap重采样估计90%置信区间）。代理均值预测作为函数评估器。

First-order ($S_1$) and total-order ($S_T$) Sobol indices~\cite{ref:sobol,ref:saltelli}
are estimated using the Jansen estimator with independent random sampling
($N_S = \num{4096}$ samples per matrix), repeated over 50~independent replications.
The mean and 90\%~confidence interval of each index are reported as the mean and
normal-approximation interval ($\bar{S} \pm 1.645\,\mathrm{SE}$) across replications,
following the variance-decomposition framework of~\cite{ref:saltelli}.
The monotone-regularized surrogate mean prediction $\mu_{\mathrm{stress}}(\bm{\theta})$ and
$\mu_{k_{\mathrm{eff}}}(\bm{\theta})$ serve as the function evaluators, enabling
sensitivity indices to be obtained at a~fraction of the cost of direct simulation~\cite{ref:zhang2025energy}.

\subsection{Observation-driven posterior calibration}\label{sec:inverse}

%CN: 后验标定：对18个基准测试案例（来自保留测试集，6 低/6 近阈值/6 高，应力 ~102–198 MPa），
%CN: 以高斯似然为条件，MCMC 更新4个标定参数（E_intercept, alpha_base, alpha_slope, nu），
%CN: 其余4个参数固定为先验均值。每案例1200个后验样本，接受率约0.49–0.59。
%CN: 另有10个高应力可行域案例（真实应力133–194 MPa）用于测试高风险区后验更新。
%CN: 后验安全分数：P_safe(τ)=P(预测应力<τ|后验)。

For the observation-driven calibration step, \textbf{18~benchmark cases} are drawn
from the held-out test split (\texttt{experiments\_0404/\_shared/fixed\_split/}),
balanced across three stress categories (6~low, 6~near-threshold, 6~high;
true stress~$\approx\SI{102}{}$--$\SI{198}{MPa}$).
A~separate set of \textbf{10~high-stress feasible-region cases}
(true stress 133--\SI{194}{MPa}, all exceeding the \SI{131}{MPa} design
threshold) is used to test posterior updating in the high-risk regime.
``Observations'' for each case are the high-fidelity outputs at the
corresponding test-split design point corrupted by additive Gaussian noise
with $\sigma_{\mathrm{obs}}=2\%$ of the output magnitude, simulating a~
realistic single-point measurement.

For each case, MCMC~\cite{ref:mcmc} with a~Gaussian likelihood is used to
update the prior over the 4-parameter calibration subset
$(E_{\mathrm{intercept}}, \alpha_{\mathrm{base}}, \alpha_{\mathrm{slope}}, \nu)$.
These are the four parameters identified by the Sobol analysis
(Section~\ref{sec:results_sobol}) as the dominant drivers of the coupled
stress and~$k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ responses plus~$\nu$, which couples the elastic
and thermal-expansion pathways.
The remaining four parameters ($E_{\mathrm{slope}}$, $T_{\mathrm{ref,SS316}}$,
$k_{\mathrm{ref,SS316}}$, $k_{\mathrm{slope,SS316}}$) are fixed at their
nominal prior-mean values, reflecting a~partial-observation scenario in which
SS316 thermal properties are treated as known from prior material
characterisation; this design choice is an acknowledged limitation discussed
in Section~\ref{sec:discussion}.

Each MCMC run generates 1200~effective posterior samples after burn-in;
measured acceptance rates fall in the range~0.49--0.59 (mean $\approx 0.55$)
across all benchmark cases.
Posterior predictions are validated against the existing HF dataset via
nearest-neighbour lookup (rather than a~true HF rerun at the posterior mean);
this constitutes a~proxy validation whose limitations are discussed in
Section~\ref{sec:discussion}.
The posterior safety fraction is defined as
\begin{equation}
  P_{\mathrm{safe}}(\tau) = P\!\left(\mu_{\mathrm{stress}}(\bm{\theta}) < \tau \mid \text{data}\right)
  = 1 - P_{\mathrm{exceed}}(\tau \mid \text{data}),
  \label{eq:psafe}
\end{equation}
estimated from the posterior predictive samples.

\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}


%CN: 结论：（1）物理正则化改善泛化（OOD alpha_base: R²从0.774→0.944）；
%CN: （2）多物理耦合压缩不确定性（应力std降约29%，keff不确定性降两个数量级以上）；
%CN: （3）独立灵敏度路径（E_intercept主控应力，alpha_base主控keff）；
%CN: （4）后验标定使10个高应力案例P_exceed落在0.60–1.0 区间（4/10达确定性）；
%CN: （5）计算加速>1300万倍。

This work presents a~probabilistic surrogate framework for coupled multi-physics
uncertainty quantification in a~heat-pipe-cooled reactor, demonstrating four
principal findings:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Physics regularization improves generalization.}
  The \texttt{data-mono-ineq} HeteroMLP, trained with combined Spearman-rank
  monotonicity and physical-inequality constraints, matches or slightly improves
  in-distribution accuracy over the unregularized baseline
  (test NLL $0.319$ vs.\ $0.407$; stress $R^2 = 0.941$ vs.\ $0.938$) while
  substantially improving OOD performance for $\alpha_{\mathrm{base}}$
  extrapolation ($R^2$: $0.774 \to 0.944$).
  This supports embedding domain knowledge as a~soft regularizer.

\item \textbf{Multi-physics coupling compresses uncertainty.}
  Forward propagation shows that coupled steady-state outputs have significantly
  tighter distributions than decoupled predictions: an approximately 29\%
  reduction in stress standard deviation and a~more than two-orders-of-magnitude
  compression of $k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ spread.
  These are consequences of the neutronics--thermal feedback mechanism and
  cannot be captured by single-physics surrogates.

\item \textbf{Distinct sensitivity pathways exist.}
  Sobol analysis identifies $E_{\mathrm{intercept}}$ as the dominant stress
  driver ($S_1 = 0.586$) and $\alpha_{\mathrm{base}}$ as the dominant
  $k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ driver ($S_1 = 0.771$), indicating that design interventions
  for stress reduction and reactivity control can be treated with largely
  separable strategies at the dominant-factor level.

\item \textbf{Posterior calibration sharpens risk assessments.}
  Bayesian calibration on 18 benchmark test cases attains a~mean 90\%~credible
  interval coverage of~$0.875$ over the four calibrated parameters; for
  10~high-stress feasible-region cases (true stress 133--\SI{194}{MPa}) the
  posterior exceedance probability $P(\sigma>\SI{131}{MPa}\mid\mathrm{obs})$
  falls in the range $0.60$--$1.00$, with 4~of~10 reaching certainty
  ($P \approx 1.00$), demonstrating that the pipeline correctly identifies
  high-risk parameter regions from observational evidence.
\end{enumerate}

The computational speedup ($>\!10^7$ over the coupled HF solver) makes this framework
practical for iterative design exploration and real-time risk monitoring in reactor
systems with significant material uncertainty.

\section{Predictive-draw risk variant and extended threshold sweep}\label{app:predictive_risk}

%CN: 附录 S2：含偶然不确定性的预测分布风险变体，并扩展到 169 / 200 MPa 两个更高阈值。
%CN: 主文使用 mu-only（仅认知不确定性）风险口径；本附录作为补充给出 predictive draw
%CN: （mu + sigma·eps，含偶然不确定性）对应的结果。
As a~supplementary uncertainty-decomposition perspective on the forward-UQ
risk analysis of Section~\ref{sec:results_uq}, we also compute a~``predictive
draw'' variant in which each Monte~Carlo sample is drawn from
$y = \mu(\bm{\theta}) + \sigma(\bm{\theta})\,\varepsilon$ with
$\varepsilon\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I)$, so that the learned aleatoric contribution
is included in addition to the epistemic sampling over~$\bm{\theta}$.
Under the same nominal-design sampling
($N=\num{20000}$, $\sigma_k=1$) as in the main text, the coupled steady-state
stress distribution has a~mean of \SI{162.6}{MPa} and a~standard deviation of
\SI{32.15}{MPa}, compared with a~mu-only standard deviation of approximately
\\SI{30.5}{MPa}; including the aleatoric term therefore widens the spread by
less than~\SI{2}{MPa} and does not overturn the risk ranking or the
qualitative conclusions reported in the main text.
Table~\ref{tab:forward_uq_appendix} extends the threshold sweep to two
higher design-relevant thresholds, $\tau=\SI{169}{MPa}$
(the MegaPower-class reported peak monolith stress under normal operating

\paragraph{Practical interpretation.}
Including the aleatoric term preserves the exceedance-probability ordering at
all five thresholds and shifts each $P_{\mathrm{exceed}}$ by less than~$0.02$;
qualitatively, the stress distribution remains concentrated above \SI{131}{MPa}
and the probability of exceeding the MegaPower reference of~\SI{169}{MPa}
remains near~$0.40$ under the nominal prior.
The main-text narrative---that the nominal design sits above the
yield-threshold on average and benefits strongly from observation-driven
posterior sharpening---is therefore robust to the choice of mu-only versus
predictive-draw sampling.
